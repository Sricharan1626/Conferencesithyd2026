Places to Visit in Hyderabad

~24 km

Statue of Equality

A 216-foot tall statue of the 11th-century Vaishnavaite saint Ramanuja. Located in Muchintal, it is one of the closest major landmarks to the conference venue, offering a spiritual and architectural marvel.

Get Directions
~45 km

Charminar

The global icon of Hyderabad, constructed in 1591. This monument and mosque is known for its intricate architecture and the bustling Laad Bazaar surrounding it, famous for pearls and bangles.

Get Directions
~48 km

Golconda Fort

A fortified citadel and an early capital city of the Qutb Shahi dynasty. Famous for its acoustic engineering, where a clap at the entrance can be heard at the highest point, a kilometer away.

Get Directions
~75 km

Ramoji Film City

Certified by Guinness World Records as the world's largest film studio complex. A fun-filled destination with live shows, thematic sets, adventure parks, and cinematic grandeur.

Get Directions
~50 km

Hussain Sagar Lake

A heart-shaped lake built by Ibrahim Quli Qutb Shah. It features a massive monolith statue of Buddha in the middle, accessible by boat. The nearby necklace road offers beautiful evening views.

Get Directions
~44 km

Chowmahalla Palace

The seat of the Asaf Jahi dynasty, this palace was where the Nizams entertained their official guests. It is renowned for its unique style, elegance, and the vintage car collection of the Nizam.

Get Directions
~38 km

Birla Temple

Birla Mandirs (temples built by the Birla family across India) are famous for their stunning white marble architecture, intricate carvings, and spiritual significance, blending traditional Indian styles (like Rajasthani, Dravidian) with modern designs, often dedicated to Lakshmi-Narayana (Vishnu & Lakshmi) or Lord Venkateswara, serving as popular pilgrimage sites and landmarks known for peace, beauty, and inclusivity.

Get Directions
~44 km

Salar Jung Museum

The Salar Jung Museum's speciality lies in its vast, eclectic collection of antiques and art from around the world, amassed by Salar Jung III, featuring world-famous pieces like the Veiled Rebecca sculpture, the intricate Cuckoo Clock, rare Jade carvings, historic Indian Manuscripts & Books, and impressive Arms & Armour, making it India's third-largest museum and a treasure trove of diverse cultures and eras.

Get Directions
~58 km

Wonderla Amusement Park

Wonderla Hyderabad is a large, popular amusement and water park on the outskirts of the city (Raviryal) offering over 40 rides and attractions, including high-thrill roller coasters like 'Recoil', water slides, a wave pool, and family rides, with dining and clean facilities, providing a full day of entertainment for all ages with varied ticket options.

Get Directions

Distances are approximate from Symbiosis Institute of Technology, Nandigama campus. Travel times may vary based on traffic.
